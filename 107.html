Appendicular skeleton
consists of bones of limbs and girdles
.
Each limb
is made of 30 bones. The bones of
the forelimbs or hands are humerus, radius and ulna, carpals (wrist bones which are 8 in number), metacarpals
(palm bones which are 5 in number) and phalanges (digits which are 14 in number).
Fig. 12. Bones of forelimbs
The bones of the hind limbs or legs are- Femur, the thigh bone (longest bone), tibia and fibula, tarsals (ankle
bones) which are 7 in number, metatarsals are 5 in number and phalanges are 14 in number. Knee cap contains
a cup shaped bone known as patella.

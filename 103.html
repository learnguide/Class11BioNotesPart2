Structure of contractile proteins
The two main contractile proteins are actin and myosin. The monomeric unit of actin is known as G actin or
globular actin. Polymers of G actin form F actin or F filaments. Two F filaments wrap around each other to
form actin molecule. A protein tropomyosin, run around the F actin. Another protein, troponin is distributed
at regular intervals on tropomyosin.
